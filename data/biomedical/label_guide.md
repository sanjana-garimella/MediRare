# Biomedical Week 1 - Label Guide

## What Counts As a Misdiagnosis Trail?

A **misdiagnosis trail** is the sequence of diagnoses a patient actually received **before** the correct diagnosis.

Do NOT include:
- a list of possible diagnoses a clinician considered (differential diagnosis)
- diseases mentioned only as background

Look for phrases like: *"initially diagnosed with"*, *"misdiagnosed as"*, *"previously treated for"*, *"presenting diagnosis was"*, *"referred after diagnosis of"*

## Diseases

### SLE (Systemic Lupus Erythematosus)

- **Common early symptoms:** Fatigue, joint pain (non-erosive, symmetric), malar rash, photosensitivity, oral ulcers, hair loss, low-grade fever, serositis (chest/abdominal pain from pleuritis or pericarditis)

- **Often misdiagnosed as:** Fibromyalgia, major depressive disorder / anxiety, rheumatoid arthritis, multiple sclerosis (when neuropsychiatric), juvenile idiopathic arthritis (in pediatric cases), IgA nephropathy (when renal-predominant)

- **Key "red flags" that suggest SLE:**
  - Positive ANA at titer ≥ 1:80, especially with anti-dsDNA or anti-Sm antibodies
  - Low complement (C3/C4) during symptomatic episodes
  - Malar rash that spares the nasolabial folds
  - Proteinuria or hematuria in a young woman with systemic symptoms
  - Symptoms affecting multiple organ systems simultaneously

- **1-2 example misdiagnosis trails:**
  - `["major depressive disorder", "fibromyalgia"]` → SLE (fatigue and diffuse pain attributed to psychiatric/functional cause for 3 years before positive ANA workup)
  - `["juvenile idiopathic arthritis", "anxiety disorder"]` → SLE (pediatric case; behavioral changes and seizures misattributed to anxiety until proteinuria and positive anti-dsDNA found)

### Sjögren's Syndrome

- **Common early symptoms:** Dry eyes (burning, foreign body sensation), dry mouth (difficulty swallowing, dental caries), fatigue, joint pain, brain fog, parotid gland swelling

- **Often misdiagnosed as:** Menopause (in perimenopausal women), depression / anxiety, fibromyalgia, medication side effects, multiple sclerosis (when CNS involvement present), seronegative rheumatoid arthritis

- **Key "red flags":**
  - Sicca symptoms (dry eyes + dry mouth) that predate or persist beyond medication use
  - Positive anti-Ro/SSA antibodies (present in ~75% of primary Sjögren's)
  - Abnormal Schirmer's test (< 5 mm/5 min) or ocular staining score
  - Symptoms not relieved by hormone replacement therapy
  - Parotid gland enlargement without infectious cause

- **1-2 example trails:**
  - `["menopausal syndrome"]` → Sjögren's (dry eyes, dry mouth, and fatigue in a 49-year-old attributed to menopause for 5 years; anti-Ro/SSA and biopsy confirmed Sjögren's)
  - `["multiple sclerosis"]` → Sjögren's (white matter lesions and cognitive dysfunction; absence of oligoclonal bands and presence of sicca symptoms led to revised diagnosis)

### MCTD (Mixed Connective Tissue Disease)

- **Common early symptoms:** Raynaud's phenomenon (almost universal; often the first symptom), puffy/swollen hands, joint pain, fatigue, muscle weakness, esophageal reflux or dysphagia

- **Often misdiagnosed as:** Undifferentiated connective tissue disease (UCTD), rheumatoid arthritis, fibromyalgia, systemic lupus erythematosus, systemic sclerosis, idiopathic pulmonary arterial hypertension (when PAH is the presenting feature)

- **Key "red flags":**
  - Raynaud's phenomenon + puffy hands is a highly specific combination for MCTD
  - High-titer anti-U1 RNP antibodies (required for diagnosis)
  - Overlap features across multiple CTDs simultaneously (arthritis + myositis + sclerodactyly)
  - Progressive exertional dyspnea in a young woman with Raynaud's (suggests PAH)
  - Proximal muscle weakness with elevated CK

- **1-2 example trails:**
  - `["rheumatoid arthritis", "fibromyalgia"]` → MCTD (symmetric arthritis and diffuse pain over 4 years; anti-U1 RNP at high titer and puffy hands eventually identified)
  - `["idiopathic pulmonary arterial hypertension"]` → MCTD (PAH as presenting feature; Raynaud's on history and anti-U1 RNP led to reclassification as MCTD-associated PAH)
